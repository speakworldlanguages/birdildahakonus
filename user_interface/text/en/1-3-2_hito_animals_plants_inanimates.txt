When you talk about the presence and absence of things in Hito language, there are two words that both mean "There is" or "It exists". <span style="text-align: left; white-space:nowrap;">いる</span> is used for people and animals while <span style="text-align: left; white-space:nowrap;">ある</span> is used for plants and inanimate things. You will now hear <span style="text-align: left; white-space:nowrap;">いる</span>.
